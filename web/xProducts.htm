Long-term Loans

Long-term loans are made for real estate purchases, debt consolidation, farm improvements, building construction and grove development and rehabilitation. Terms: Generally, longer than 10 years. Long-term loans require a first lien on real estate security.

Intermediate-term Loans

Intermediate-term loans are made for purchases of machinery, equipment, vehicles, breeding stock and real estate; farm and home improvements; grove development and rehabilitation; construction of livestock and poultry facilities; and debt consolidation. Terms: Up to 10 years.

Short-term Loans

Short-term loans are made for general operating expenses such as labor, feed, seed, fertilizer, grove caretaking, repairs, veterinary costs and small capital purchases. Inventory and commodity loans are made for the purpose of marketing. Terms: Generally, one year or within an operating business cycle. Operating and line of credit loans require crop liens in addition to other underlying security.

Correspondent Lending for Rural Home Loans

AgFirst Farm Credit Bank, a Farmer Mac certified facility and Fannie Mae approved lender/servicer, has established a mortgage loan purchase program for rural home loans. AgFirst is a financial institution created and charged by Congress to provide credit for housing in rural areas, as well as credit to farmers and ranchers. Therefore, our experience and expertise in working with Rural America lends itself well to understanding the unique residential underwriting issues associated with the rural location, property size and/or agricultural or mixed-use type property characteristics. AgFirst expects to be the leader--in providing residential financing in Rural America.

Conventional Rural Home Loan Program

The homeowner desiring to live in Rural America now has long term fixed rate financing available. The property’s primary use must be residential; however, income may be generated from a small farming operation. 

Key Criteria for Eligible Rural Home Loans:

  • Single-family, owner-occupied, detached residence located on the property.

  • As a guideline, the contributory value of the Dwelling should represent 50% or more of the total appraised value.

  • Property must be located in a rural area or in a town or municipality having a population of 2,500 or less inhabitants.

  • Private Mortgage Insurance is required on loans with a Loan-to-Value greater than 80%.

  • Maximum Loan-to-Value is 95%.

  • Loan must be secured by a first lien.

  • Escrow for taxes and insurance required on loans with a Loan-to-Value greater than 80%.

Advantages of Our Conventional Rural Home Loans:

  • Competitively priced conventional interest rate products available.

  • Standard residential mortgage documents and credit underwriting criteria used.

  • Monthly payments.

  • No pre-payment penalty.

  • Maximum Loan Amount-$417,000

Home Loans for the Rural Homeowner:

  • Property may be used for both residential and agricultural purposes.

  • No maximum acreage limitation.

  • Property may be accessed by a private road.

  • Easements may be permissible provided such easements do not render the property unmarketable.

  • Manufactured Housing acceptable (double-wide and larger).

Part-time Farm/Residental Loan Program

Residential mortgage financing in Rural America may involve a homeowner conducting or planning to conduct a small farming operation. The homeowner desiring to live in Rural America now has conventional  financing available for the property with both a residence and a small farming operation.

Key Criteria for Eligible Rural Home Loans:

  • Single-family, detached residence located on the property, may be either owner-occupied or a second home.

  • As a guideline, the contributory value of the Dwelling should represent 30% or more of the total appraised value. Collateral will normally consist of acreage tracts, of which a portion must be capable of agricultural production. If, the collateral consists of less than 5 acres, a minimum of $5,000 gross agricultural income must be produced annually;

                                    OR

  • If, the collateral consists of 5 acres or more, there is no minimum gross agricultural income requirement; however, the collateral must be capable of agricultural production.

  • Private Mortgage Insurance is required on loans with a Loan-to-Value greater than 80%

  • Maximum Loan-to-Value is 85%.

  • Loan must be secured by a first lien.

  • Leasehold property eligible.

  • Multiple and non-contiguous real estate parcels allowed as security.

  • Escrow for taxes and insurance required on loans with a Loan-to-Value greater than 80%.

Advantages of Our Part-time Farm/Residential Loans:

  • Competitively priced interest rate products available.

  • Standard residential mortgage documents and credit underwriting criteria used.

  • Monthly payments.

  • No pre-payment penalty.

  • Maximum Loan Amount $3,000,000.

Home Loans for the Part-time Farmer:

  • Property used for both residential and agricultural purposes.

  • No maximum acreage limitation.

  • Property may be accessed by a private road.

  • Easements may be permissible provided such easements do not render the property unmarketable.

  • Manufactured Housing acceptable (double-wide and larger).

Farm-Related Business Loans

Farm-related business loans are made to an individual or legal entity engaged in furnishing custom-type farm-related services related to on-farm operating needs. Loans purposes include sites, capital structures, equipment and working capital. Terms: Loan term corresponds with the normal useful life of the asset financed.

Equipment Leasing

Farm Credit specializes in leasing agricultural transportation (including pick-up trucks) and material handling equipment. Lease schedules are usually five years but may range from three to ten years depending upon the type of equipment. Payments may be made monthly, quarterly, semi-annually, annually or on a harvest payment plan. All eligible borrowers for Farm Credit loans may lease equipment. Lease payments are generally tax deductible. At the end of the lease term, you may extend the lease, return the equipment or purchase it according to the terms of the contract.
