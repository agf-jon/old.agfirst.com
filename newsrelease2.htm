AGFIRST BOARD OF DIRECTORS RECEIVE “KEYS TO THE CITY”
FROM COLUMBIA MAYOR BOB COBLE

Columbia, S.C. - Columbia Mayor Bob Coble presented the "Keys to the City" to the AgFirst Farm Credit Bank board of directors Monday, March 19, 2001, in recognition of AgFirst's commitment to public art in the city of Columbia.

"Public art enriches our community and our economy," Mayor Coble commented. "AgFirst has shown leadership in the support of public art since 1975, when it commissioned 'Tunnelvision,' a mural which has made the bank's building a landmark in the city," he added.

Merrell Lust, chairman of the AgFirst board, accepted the recognition on behalf of the board of directors and the bank, saying, "We are delighted to contribute to the cultural growth of the city by providing works of art for the enjoyment of its residents and visitors. We've been committed to agriculture for more than 80 years, and we're committed to the city of Columbia, as well."

AgFirst, formerly known as the Federal Land Bank and Federal Intermediate Credit Bank of Columbia, has been located in Columbia since 1917. The bank held a rededication ceremony in February 2000 to celebrate its history and communicate to the city its dedication to art and agriculture.

"We are grateful to be recognized by Mayor Coble for our public art, as well as our other contributions to the City of Columbia," said AgFirst CEO F. A. (Andy) Lowrey. "We look forward to continuing and strengthening our partnership with the city," he added.

AgFirst began its support of the arts in 1975 with the Tunnelvision mural on the back the building and continued in 2000 with a mural on an adjacent building entitled "Light at the End of the Tunnel." Both murals are creations of artist Blue Sky. The latest artwork, a larger-than-life-size fire hydrant sitting slightly askew and spewing water, is what spurred the city to bestow this honor upon the AgFirst board of directors.

AgFirst is part of the nationwide Farm Credit System, the largest agricultural lending organization in the United States. The bank provides funding and financial services to 24 farmer-owned financial cooperatives in 15 eastern states and Puerto Rico.
