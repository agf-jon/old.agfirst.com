AgFirst Announces Earnings of $90 Million in 2000

Columbia, S.C. -- AgFirst Farm Credit Bank today reported net income of $90 million for the year ended December 31, 2000, up from $87 million the previous year.  Growth in loan volume and a reduction in noninterest expenses contributed to the increase in earnings.

The bank reported that its loan portfolio grew by more than 10 percent in 2000, the result of an increase in loans originated by its affiliated retail lenders, as well as loans purchased through its capital markets and secondary mortgage market units.  Loan volume stood at $9.5 billion on December 31, 2000, up from $8.6 billion the previous year.

AgFirst is part of the nationwide Farm Credit System, the largest agricultural lending organization in the United States. The bank provides funding and financial services to 24 farmer-owned financial cooperatives in 15 eastern states and Puerto Rico.  These cooperatives, called Agricultural Credit Associations (ACAs), provide real estate and production financing to more than 79,000 farmers, rural homeowners and agri-businesses..

Since 1989, ACAs affiliated with AgFirst have paid patronage dividends of more than $1.2 billion to their farmer-borrowers.  "Paying patronage dividends and improving stockholder value are the drivers behind a successful cooperative," said AgFirst CEO F. A. (Andy) Lowrey.  "AgFirst and its affiliated associations have a long and impressive history of returning value to their stockholders through patronage dividends," he added.  "Our volume growth is a reflection of that impressive patronage dividend history." 

The bank, which is the largest financial institution headquartered in South Carolina, reported total assets of $11.9 billion at December 31, 2000, an increase of more than $723 million from year-end 1999.   Nationally, the Farm Credit System reported combined assets of $94 billion on December 31, 2000, as compared to $88.7 billion a year ago.  The System's combined net income for the year ended December 31, 2000 was $1.42 billion, as compared to $1.23 billion the previous year.
