AgFirst Official Outlines Capital Markets' Role
Felicia Morant, Vice President of Capital Markets
for AgFirst Bank

Felicia Morant joined AgFirst Farm Credit Bank in Columbia in August as vice president/capital markets.

Morant, 32, spent five years with Bank of America and predecessor NationsBank, where she was a middle-market commercial lender, moving from Raleigh, N.C. to Columbia in 1999.  With Bank of America, she worked with corporations with annual revenues of between $15 million and $100 million.

AgFirst, with more than $13 billion in assets, is a member of the nationwide Farm Credit System, a $90 billion network of cooperative lending institutions and a key player in agricultural financing.

The State recently asked Morant about her move to AgFirst and her outlook for the company.

What do you do as vice president of capital markets?

I’m a business development officer.  I establish and manage relationships with financial institutions that are involved in loan participations and syndications.  These include commercial banks, insurance companies and financial institutions in the farm credit system and any lender dedicated to agricultural lending.

What’s your typical work day like?

I have no typical work day.  I travel frequently throughout the country in my job.  I meet with banks, insurance companies and other organizations with units dedicated to lending to agribusinesses to familiarize them with AgFirst’s capital markets capabilities, as well as to learn about potential loan opportunities.

How did your past experience prepare you for this position?

A:  My career path was a non-traditional one for a commercial lender.  I began working in banking with NationsBank as what is now known as a personal banker, opening accounts at a branch.  After exceeding the branch’s annual sales goals within a matter of months, I felt like I needed a larger challenge.

The bank then allowed me to move into middle-market commercial lending.  While the arena is different, the role of business development officer is a natural next step from being a commercial lending officer.

My academic background wasn’t heavily influenced by banking and finance. I have a bachelor’s degree in psychology.  I understand people and am a natural salesperson, and if I believe in the benefit of a product or service then I can make a customer believe as well.  That’s been the key to my success.

What role does AgFirst’s capital markets unit play in the company’s operation?

The capital markets unit is responsible for serving food and agribusiness companies in the United States whose credit transactions require shared credit risk with other financial institutions.

If I’m an AgFirst customer – a farmer, landowner or someone else involved in agribusiness – what does your job mean to me?

My job ultimately benefits their bottom line.  The earnings generated by the capital markets unit are shared with the Farm Credit associations affiliated with AgFirst.  These associations, in turn, share their earnings with their farmer-borrowers through patronage dividends, thus reducing the borrowing costs for those farmers, landowners and others.
